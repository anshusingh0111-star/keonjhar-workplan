AECC rollout workplan — Keonjhar district

27 milestones · 4 phases · M1 (month 1) through Q12 (approx. month 30)

Phase 0 · M1–M3
Prerequisite setup
Months 1–3 (approx. 3 months)
Pure groundwork — no services go live. Every workstream is in setup mode: governance structures formed, all RFPs drafted and issued, physical and HR baselines done, 12 policies drafted, civil works and equipment deployment completed, and ABHA validation begins. By end of M3 the district has a functioning PMU, a signed Apollo MoU, vendors shortlisted, and CCC space established.
State approval & DMF funds committed
12/12 policies drafted
All 3 vendors (CCC/HIS/PHR) onboarded
Civil works & equipment deployment complete
M&E framework finalized
Phase 1 · Q2–Q4
1 CHC Padampur + 5 sub-centres go live
Quarters 2–4 (approx. 6–9 months)
Services go live at Padampur CHC and 5 sub-centres. HIS declared live, CCC becomes operational, Apollo 66 centres activate, RAG dashboard is tested and live by Q4. ABHA registration and ASHA orientation run intensively. First real citizen touchpoints — slot booking, CFC booths, teleconsultation — become functional. STG adherence testing begins.
HIS & CCC live (Q2–Q3)
Apollo 66 centres aligned with CCM (Q3)
RAG dashboard functioning (Q4)
Citizen alerts go live (Q3)
PHWS scorecards published (Q2)
Phase 2 · Q5–Q8
Full Keonjhar district
Quarters 5–8 (approx. 9–18 months)
Scale-out across the district. All infra rollouts repeat at new facilities (booths, HIS, CFC, slot management). Additional 34 Apollo centres go live. CPE and prescription audits become routine. Knowledge products begin development. Swasti recruits start transitioning — full-time PMU is funded by Dept/DMF by Q5. Inter-block competition designed.
Full-time internal PMU funded (Q5)
34 additional Apollo centres (Q5–Q6)
CPE & prescription audits running (Q6–Q7)
Knowledge products in development (Q8)
Dashboards institutionalized (Q8+)
Phase 3 · Q9–Q13
Stabilization & phasing out
Quarters 9–13 (approx. 18–30 months)
Swasti steps back; PMU runs fully on internal staff by Q10. Monitoring, refresher campaigns and biannual PHWS scorecards continue. Knowledge transfer formally completed (Q12). The program connects to State CCM systems and Swasti exits. Routine governance — steering meetings, STG reviews, audits, dashboard use — is entirely district-owned.
PMU fully internal, Swasti phased out (Q10)
Biannual PHWS & inter-block competition active
Knowledge transfer & handover done (Q12)
State CCM system connection (Q12)

📋 12 Key Policies — to be drafted & implemented

  • 1Omni-channel access
  • 2Teleconsult through booths, with no turn-back policy
  • 3Panel of doctors for teleconsult onboarded with pricing approved (public and private) — for public sector doctors, remuneration to be provided if outside office hours
  • 4No denial of service irrespective of ABHA status
  • 5Alternate IDs used for non-ABHA situations
  • 6Clear pathway for different ID availability in different situations
  • 7Consent guidelines developed and implemented (AV consent suggested)
  • 8'Own your phone' policy and provision of appropriate mobile phones to frontline workers and all caregivers
  • 9Pre-reading of patient-related information by provider before meeting
  • 10Information sharing guidelines in facility between individual doctors, teams, nurses and the department
  • 11Hardware and software available
  • 12Filling up of all vacant positions
